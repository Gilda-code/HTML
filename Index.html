Trading Knowledge

Here are some key points to remember

Before buying a stock:

  • Candles: Analyze candlestick patterns to understand price action, reversals, and trends.
  • Trend: Identify the direction of the market (uptrend, downtrend, or sideways) over time.
  • Volume: Look at trading volume to confirm price movements and assess the strength of a trend.
  • 20-Day Moving Average: Use this short-term moving average to smooth out price data and identify trends.
  • Gaps: Monitor price gaps (when a stock opens significantly higher or lower than its previous close) to signal strong market sentiment or reversals.
  • Support and Resistance: Identify key price levels where the stock tends to reverse or consolidate.
  • CCI (+100/-100): Use the Commodity Channel Index to identify overbought (+100) or oversold (-100) conditions, signaling potential buy or sell points.
  • Bearish/Bullish: Determine whether the overall sentiment is bullish (rising) or bearish (falling).
  • Stop Loss: Set a predefined price at which you’ll sell the stock to minimize losses if the price moves against you.
  • Take Profit: Define a target price at which you’ll sell to lock in gains once your trade has moved in your favor.

The Human factor

The emotional factor in investing plays a critical role, often leading to impulsive decisions that can undermine a well-thought-out strategy. Fear and greed are the two dominant emotions that can cloud judgment—fear may push investors to sell in panic during market downturns, while greed can cause overconfidence and risky bets during market highs. Managing emotions, staying disciplined, and adhering to long-term goals are essential to avoiding costly mistakes driven by short-term market fluctuations. Emotional control is just as important as financial analysis when it comes to successful investing.
